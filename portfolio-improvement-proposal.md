## Key Changes

### 1. **Add a Long-Form "About" / Biography Page**

This is your SEO anchor. Saraev's biography ranks for terms like "AI automation agency" because it tells a story while naturally including keywords.

**Your version could include:**
- Your background and how you got into tech/design/whatever your focus is
- Projects and their outcomes (with specifics: "built X that did Y")
- Your philosophy/approach
- Skills and tools you work with
- What you're looking for (consulting, interesting projects, collaborations)

**Word count target**: 2,000-4,000 words. This isn't vanity ‚Äî it's surface area for Google.

---

### 2. **Start a "Writing" Section**

Not a newsletter. Just searchable articles on topics you know well.

**Content types that work for SEO + demonstrate expertise:**

| Type | Example Topics |
|------|----------------|
| How-to guides | "How to [do thing you're good at]" |
| Case studies | "How I built [project] for [client/self]" |
| Tool comparisons | "[Tool A] vs [Tool B] for [use case]" |
| Problem/solution posts | "Why [common approach] fails and what to do instead" |
| Build logs | "Building [side project]: Week 1" |

**Frequency**: Even 1-2 posts per month creates compounding SEO value.

---

### 3. **Expand Project Pages into Case Studies**

Current portfolio sites usually show: Screenshot + title + maybe a link.

Better approach: Each project gets its own page with:
- The problem/context
- Your approach
- The outcome (metrics if possible)
- Tech used
- What you learned

This makes each project a searchable, indexable asset.

---

### 4. **Add a Services Page**

Even if you're not actively selling, having a `/services` page:
- Tells Google what you do
- Gives inbound visitors a clear "here's how to work with me"
- Can link to Upwork profile or contact form

Keep it simple:
- 2-3 service offerings
- What's included
- How to get started (email/call/Upwork)

---

### 5. **Soft CTAs Everywhere (Not Cringey)**

You don't need pop-ups or "SUBSCRIBE NOW" banners.

Instead, every page ends with something like:

> **Want to work together?**  
> I take on consulting projects and interesting builds.  
> [Email me](mailto:you@email.com) ¬∑ [LinkedIn](link) ¬∑ [Upwork](link)

Or for articles:

> **More writing**: [Link to /writing]  
> **About me**: [Link to /about]

## Design Recommendations

Based on the frontend-design skill and avoiding "AI slop":

### Typography
- **Avoid**: Inter, Roboto, system fonts
- **Consider**: JetBrains Mono (for code aesthetic), Newsreader (editorial), IBM Plex (technical/clean)

### Color
- Pick a bold direction: dark mode with high contrast, or light with a single strong accent
- Avoid purple gradients on white (the AI clich√©)

### Layout
- Use generous whitespace
- Consider asymmetric layouts or grid-breaking elements for personality
- Let the content breathe

### Motion
- Subtle page-load animations (staggered reveals)
- Hover states that feel intentional
- Don't overdo it
